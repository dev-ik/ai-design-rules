# Research Library

Research is the first step in AI Design Rules.

Use product research to observe how strong products reduce effort, organize repeated actions, and make complex behavior feel simple. Do not turn research into rules until the observation is stable and reusable.

## Pipeline Position

```text
observation -> research -> knowledge -> rules -> patterns -> prompts -> reference projects -> reviews
```

Research files should feed later rules and patterns. They are not final rules.

See `docs/KNOWLEDGE_ENGINE.md` for object metadata and relationship standards.

Manual indexes are transitional. Future indexes should be generated from registry metadata.

## Product Studies

- `products/telegram.md` - speed, optimistic interaction, chat-scale IA.
- `products/things-3.md` - calm task management, progressive disclosure, tactile list editing.
- `products/apple-reminders.md` - simple lists, system integration, low-friction capture.
- `products/linear.md` - perceived performance, workflow structure, operational clarity.
- `products/arc.md` - workspace organization, context switching, browser interaction model.

## Research Format

Each product note should include:

- why this product is studied;
- official source signals;
- observed design behavior;
- design takeaways for agents;
- candidate rule directions;
- what not to copy blindly.

## Contribution Rule

Add research before adding rules. A rule without evidence is likely just taste.
