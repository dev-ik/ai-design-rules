# Arc

## Why Study It

Arc is useful for studying workspace organization, context switching, browser interaction models, and motion-led product feel.

## Source Signals

- Arc positions itself as a browser that organizes work, study, hobbies, and other contexts in one window.
- Arc uses Spaces and Profiles for context separation.
- Arc includes Split View, Themes, Pinned Tabs, Favorites, Peek, Little Arc, Mini Player, Boosts, Easels, and sharing for spaces, folders, and splits.
- Arc's site notes that Arc now receives Chromium updates only and points users who need active security patches to Dia.

Sources:

- https://arc.net/
- https://resources.arc.net/hc/en-us/categories/16435255982103-Features

## Observed Design Behavior

- The browser is treated as a workspace, not only a page viewer.
- Context is organized spatially through Spaces, Profiles, pinned tabs, favorites, and split views.
- Lightweight preview patterns reduce full context switching.
- Creation tools sit inside browsing instead of requiring a separate app.
- Motion and interaction polish communicate that the product is meant to feel different from a default browser.

## Design Takeaways For Agents

- For products with many contexts, make the context model visible and navigable.
- Reduce context switches with previews, split surfaces, and temporary windows.
- Use motion to preserve orientation, not to decorate.
- Workspace products need opinionated organization defaults.

## Candidate Rule Directions

- Context-heavy products should expose context boundaries clearly.
- Preview interactions should help users inspect without committing to navigation.
- Motion should explain where objects came from and where they went.

## Do Not Copy Blindly

- Do not copy Arc's novelty if the product's value depends on predictability.
- Do not use motion-heavy interactions without accessibility and performance review.
