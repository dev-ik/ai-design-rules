# UX-003 Preserve Context During Inspection

Status: Draft
Domain: UX

## Source Research

- `research/products/arc.md`
- `research/products/linear.md`
- `research/products/telegram.md`

## Why

Users lose momentum when every inspection forces a full navigation change. Preview, split, and side-panel patterns reduce context switching when users need to inspect without committing.

## Rule

When users need to compare, inspect, or triage items, provide a way to view detail without losing the original context.

## Good

- Detail can open in a preview, side panel, split view, or temporary surface.
- Returning to the list or workspace preserves scroll, filters, and selection.
- Motion or placement makes it clear where the detail came from.

## Bad

- Opening detail resets the list, filters, or scroll position.
- Users must navigate back and rebuild context after every inspection.
- Preview surfaces become full editors with too many actions.

## Review

- Identify inspection-heavy workflows.
- Open and close item detail repeatedly.
- Confirm the source context remains intact.
- Confirm the preview does not hide essential primary actions.
