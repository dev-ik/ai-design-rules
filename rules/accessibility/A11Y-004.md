# A11Y-004 Reduce Non-Essential Interaction Motion

Status: Draft
Domain: Accessibility

## Source Research

- `research/accessibility/keyboard-focus-and-interaction-motion.md`

## Why

Non-essential interaction motion can distract users or cause dizziness, nausea, and headaches. A product must not make that motion a requirement for understanding or operating the flow.

## Rule

When an interaction triggers non-essential motion, respect a reduced-motion preference or provide an equivalent control that disables the motion. Preserve the same information and function in the non-moving state.

## Good

- A sheet opens immediately when reduced motion is enabled while keeping the same focus and close behavior.
- Decorative parallax or list movement is disabled without hiding content.
- A transition that explains state also has a static end-state presentation.

## Bad

- A user must watch a sliding or zooming transition to know what opened.
- Reduced motion removes a required control or information instead of only non-essential movement.
- Decorative motion is added without a way to avoid it.

## Review

- List interaction-triggered motion separately from essential scrolling and required information.
- Enable a reduced-motion preference or control and verify the task remains complete.
- Confirm loading and error feedback remain visible without motion.
