# PRD-001 Daily Actions First

Status: Draft
Domain: Product

## Source Research

- `research/products/things-3.md`
- `research/products/telegram.md`
- `research/products/apple-reminders.md`

## Why

Consumer products earn trust through repeated daily actions. If the most common action is buried under structure, decoration, or setup, the product feels heavier than the job it solves.

## Rule

Design the main screen around the user's most repeated action before adding secondary navigation, advanced metadata, or decorative sections.

## Good

- The primary repeated action is visible without scrolling.
- The action can be completed without opening a configuration flow.
- Supporting state is quiet and does not compete with the action.
- Secondary tools are available but not placed ahead of the daily job.

## Bad

- The screen starts with analytics, cards, onboarding copy, or branding while the daily action is hidden.
- A simple action requires choosing categories, filters, or settings first.
- The interface optimizes for edge cases before the repeated behavior.

## Review

- Name the most repeated action for this product.
- Confirm the action is reachable in the first viewport.
- Confirm secondary actions do not visually dominate it.
- Confirm the rule still fits products where the primary job is review, not creation.
