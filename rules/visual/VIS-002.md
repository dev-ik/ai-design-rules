# VIS-002 Keep Expression Subordinate To Content

Status: Draft
Domain: Visual

## Source Research

- `research/visual/expressive-system-ui-2026.md`

## Why

Materials, strong shapes, color, typography, and depth can create product identity, but equal or excessive emphasis obscures content and primary actions. Modern platform systems use expression selectively and preserve semantic hierarchy.

## Rule

Use the strongest expressive treatment only where it clarifies functional hierarchy, primary emphasis, or product identity. Keep core content readable and dominant, and encode repeated expression through semantic system roles.

## Good

- Navigation and controls are visually distinct from the content they operate on.
- One focal treatment identifies the primary action or important state.
- Typography, shape, material, color, and depth use reusable semantic roles.
- The interface remains understandable when transparency, motion, or decorative effects are reduced.

## Bad

- Every card, control, and background uses the same strong material or effect.
- Glass, gradients, shadows, or unusual shapes compete with reading and task completion.
- A platform-specific effect is copied to unrelated platforms without fallback.
- Removing decorative effects collapses hierarchy or hides the primary action.

## Review

- Identify the content layer, functional layer, focal accent, and quiet supporting surfaces.
- Temporarily remove decorative effects and confirm content order and primary action remain clear.
- Check high contrast, reduced transparency, and reduced motion where relevant.
- Confirm repeated visual decisions map to semantic tokens or component roles.
