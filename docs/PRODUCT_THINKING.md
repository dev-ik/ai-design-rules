# Product Thinking

Product thinking comes before interface generation.

Before an agent proposes UI, it should understand:

- who the user is;
- what job they are trying to complete;
- what they repeat often;
- what must be fast, calm, or trustworthy;
- what can wait until later.

## Agent Questions

- What is the user's primary goal in this product?
- What action happens every day or every session?
- What object is the product built around?
- What must be visible immediately?
- What should be progressively disclosed?
- What state can block user progress?

## Output Standard

A product decision should be specific enough to become a rule, pattern, prompt, benchmark criterion, or review finding.

Avoid vague claims such as "make it clean" or "make it modern" unless they are translated into concrete behavior.
